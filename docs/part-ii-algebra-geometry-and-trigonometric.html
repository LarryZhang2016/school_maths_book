0.2 Solving equations with two unknowns

Letâ€™s try to solve the this problem:

Alan thought two numbers and he asked Alice to guess what they are. Alan told Alice that the sum of the two numbers are 100 and the difference between the two numbers is 36. Can you help Alice figure out the two numbers?

Analysis: We must find the two numbers, which are unknowns to us. So, why not let the two unknowns be \(x\) and \(y\). Immediately, we have the following two equations \[ \left\{ \begin{array}{ccl} x + y &=& 100\\ x - y &=& 36 \end{array} \right. \] Now, if we can solve the two equations then we solve the problem.

This chapter is about solving equations with two unknowns. By that, I mean we want to find \(x\) and \(y\) in equations like the above one. The method for solving this type of equations are called elimination method; as the name indicates the key is that we firstly knock out one unknown and then we get the other one.

For the above the problem, we can knock out \(y\) be adding the two equations together \[ 2x = 136, \] from whch easily we have \(x=\frac{136}{2}=68\). Next, to find \(y\), we can either replace 68 for \(x\) in one equation to have \(68+y=100\), which yields \(y=32\), or we this time knock out \(y\) by using the top equation to substract the second equation \[ 2y = 64, \] from which again we get \(y=32\).
